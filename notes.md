# The PP Good Pass Map — Data Notes

**Data collected:** April 2026
**Measure:** % of pupils achieving grade 5+ in both GCSE English Language and GCSE Maths ("good pass")
**Breakdown:** Disadvantaged vs. non-disadvantaged vs. all pupils

---

## Key Definitions

**Disadvantaged pupils** (DfE definition): Pupils known to have been eligible for free school meals (FSM) at any point in the past six years (Year 6 to Year 11), OR recorded as looked after for at least one day, OR adopted from care. This is a broader definition than simply "current FSM" — it is the standard used in KS4 performance statistics and for pupil premium funding.

**Note:** The term "PP" (pupil premium) in practice maps to this "ever-FSM 6 years" definition in secondary school performance data. Direct FSM-only breakdowns (current eligibility only) are published separately and will show slightly different numbers.

**Good pass:** Grade 5 or above in both English Language and Maths GCSE. This is the government's "strong pass" measure, distinct from the grade 4+ "standard pass."

## Data Availability Notes

- **Pre-2018-19:** The grade 5+ measure by disadvantage status is not consistently available in published summary statistics before 2018-19. The grade 9-1 system was introduced in 2017 (English and maths first), but early releases focused on other headline measures. The DfE's underlying data tables (downloadable CSVs) may contain 2016-17 and 2017-18 breakdowns but these were not accessible via web search at time of collection.
- **2016-17 and 2017-18 gap:** The data tables at explore-education-statistics.service.gov.uk contain downloadable CSVs that should include these years. Worth downloading directly to extend the time series backwards.
- **2019-20 to 2021-22:** COVID years. Centre-assessed and teacher-assessed grades inflated all figures substantially. These years are **not comparable** with pre- and post-pandemic exam years and should be treated as a break in the series or shown separately.
- **2024-25:** Provisional data published but the specific percentage breakdown for disadvantaged vs. non-disadvantaged has not been published at time of data collection (April 2026). The disadvantage gap index is reported as 3.92 (down slightly from 3.94 in 2023-24).

## The Key Numbers (exam years only)

| Year | Disadvantaged | Non-disadvantaged | All pupils | Gap |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----------|-----|
| 2018-19 | 24.8% | 50.0% | 43.4% | 25.2 pp |
| 2022-23 | 25.4% | 52.6% | 45.5% | 27.2 pp |
| 2023-24 | 26.0% | 53.4% | 46.2% | 27.4 pp |

The gap has **widened by ~2 percentage points** since 2018-19. The Schools White Paper (February 2026) sets a national ambition to **halve this gap** — implying a target of around 13-14 percentage points, which would require either disadvantaged pupils rising to ~40% or a combination of improvement and narrowing.
